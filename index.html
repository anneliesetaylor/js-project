The Origin of Coffee

by Anneliese Taylor

What is Coffee?

Coffee is a refreshment made from brewing roasted coffee beans. Served either hot or iced, the dark colored drink is slightly bitter in taste due to its acidic nature. It is one of the most popular drinks in the world as a result of the stimulating effects it can produce from its caffeine content. The bitterness of the beverage is often counteracted by the addition of cream, milk, or other dairy substitutes in addition to sugar or alternative sweeteners.

How is Coffee Made?

Coffee beans are derived from the seeds of berries from the Coffea plant species. The berries from the Coffea plant change in color from green to bright red to indicate they are ripe, allowing for them to be picked and processed in order to be dried. Once the beans have been roasted, these dried coffee seeds are what we know as coffee beans. The duration and temperature of the roasting process is determined by the desired flavor of the coffee, with varying degrees and timeframes impacting the flavor profile of the coffee beans when brewed.

Espresso, one of the most popular ways of brewing coffee beans, is a method of brewing that originated from Italy and uses a minimal amount of boiling water forced through finely-ground coffee beans using pressure. The process results in a small amount of thicker, more concentrated coffee.

Different Types of Espresso Beverages

Latte

A Latte is a coffee drink made with espresso and steamed milk. It is made using a ratio of one third espresso to two thirds milk, and it can be served either hot or iced.

Image by Nathan Dumlao from Unsplash.

Cappuccino

A Cappuccino is an espresso-based coffee drink made with steamed milk foam, or sometimes cream. It is smaller than a latte and often features a thicker layer of milk foam on top.

Image by Fahmi Fakhrudin from Unsplash.

Macchiato

A Macchiato is an espresso coffee drink made with a small amount of foamed milk. The name for the beverage is Italian, translating to "stained" or "spotted".
